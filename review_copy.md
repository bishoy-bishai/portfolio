# REVIEW: My Biggest Mistake: Why You Should i18n Your Next.js App From Day One (A Vibe Coding Survival Guide)

**Primary Tech:** NextJS

## 🎥 Video Script
Hey everyone! Let me tell you about one of my biggest "facepalm" moments in development. It was on a really exciting Next.js project, a SaaS platform with global ambitions. We were flying, shipping features like crazy, feeling like rockstars. Then, inevitably, the client dropped the bomb: "Okay, time for internationalization! We need Spanish, German, Japanese..."

My heart sank. I remember thinking, "Why didn't we just do this from day one?" Retrofitting i18n was a nightmare. Every single string, every dynamic piece of content, every date format – it was a cascading change that touched almost every component. The estimates tripled, the sprint velocity plummeted, and our codebase became a tangle of conditional rendering and string lookups.

Here's the thing I learned the hard way: internationalizing your Next.js app *from day one* isn't just good practice; it's a survival strategy. Next.js offers fantastic routing for i18n out of the box, and integrating a library like `next-i18next` early on is a fraction of the cost of fixing it later. Trust me, future you will thank present you for making this seemingly small, but monumentally impactful, decision. Your global audience and your sanity depend on it.
